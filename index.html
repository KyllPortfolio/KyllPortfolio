Prayer of a Student Teacher



Lord Jesus, when you lived and worked and talked amongst men in Palestine, they call you
Teacher.

Help me to remember the greatness of the work which has been given to me to do: that I work
with the most precious material in the world, the mind of youth. Help me always to remember
that I am making marks upon that mind which time will never rub out.

Give me patience with those who are slow to learn, and even with those who refuse to learn.
When I have to exercise discipline, help me to do so in sternness and yet in love. Keep me from
the sarcastic and the biting tongue and help me always to encourage those who are doing their
best, even if that best is not very good.

Help me to help my students, not only to store things in their memories, but to be able to use their minds, and to think for themselves. And amidst all the worries and the frustrations of my job, help me to remember that the future of the nation and the world is in my hands. Amen.

SYNOPSIS OF PROFESSIONAL READINGS AND REFERENCES

Foundations of Education by Jose F. Calderon

Chapter 6: Motivation

An essential component of learning is motivation. Learning will either not happen at all or not occur very much without motivation. While there are many ways to define motivation, they all boil down to arousing a learner's attention and steering them to achieve the objective. It is a process of generating and maintaining interest in an activity. Additionally, it involves the learner developing a motivation to complete a task to meet a need. In general, younger students like the elementary students are not as aware of how important it is to execute their tasks correctly. To accomplish the intended goals, the teacher must supply the essential motivators.

Importance of Motivation according to J. Calderon

1. Directs activities towards the achievement of a goal.

- Motivation inspires students to pursue their goals with tenacity and directs behaviors toward particular goals. 2. Controls and directs human behavior.

- It governs decisions and deeds, molding student’s behavior and personality by encouraging some behaviors and discouraging others.

3. Inculcates spiritual and moral values in the minds of the young
- Motivation encourages students to follow moral standards and develop empathy. It also builds integrity and makes a positive contribution to society.

4. Gives satisfaction and happiness to the individual.
- Pursuing meaningful goals is a source of motivation that enhances student’s lives and improves their general well-being.

In summary, fostering effective instruction and enabling the best possible learning outcomes in primary school requires a thorough grasp of student motivation. Teachers may create a dynamic and engaging learning environment that stimulates curiosity, cultivates intrinsic or extrinsic motivation, and equips students to become lifelong learners by acknowledging and accommodating the varied motivations of their students. Teachers may harness the power of student motivation to foster academic success, personal development, and a lifetime love of learning by using deliberate and purposeful instructional approaches. Becoming an effective educator is one of my aims as an inspiring teacher. It can be done by empowering the pupils to achieve more and build positive relationships with everyone despite their disparate reasons.

As I begin teaching in the real setting, it is one of my struggles. Despite knowing the content and the flow of my lesson, it still boils down to how I will motivate my pupils in our discussion. Because the topic is already given which can be found in the Curriculum Guide or Most Essential Learning Competencies (MELCs) it is up to you to decide how to present it. You have to teach what the assigned topic is that week. So, you have to scan the MELCs on that subject before making a lesson plan.

Journal

Week 1

As I commenced my week in the grade 2 classroom, I was reintroduced by my cooperating teacher to the class and informed the children that I would begin teaching them. As they recalled, during my field study, we were primarily there to observe. This is why the adviser emphasized my role to the pupils for the upcoming weeks. My cooperating teacher offered me a choice of which subjects I would like to teach, and I insisted on trying all of them. After teaching, the teacher would provide feedback on areas for improvement and things to avoid.

Week 2

In my third rotation, I was assigned to the first section of grade 6. The learners there were brilliant and disciplined. They were already mature, so handling them wasn't too difficult. Just like my co-student teacher said, a single word was enough for them. Early in the morning, they went to their assigned area to clean without waiting for instructions. It seemed like they already knew what to do because they were in the star section. They were hospitable and caring towards student interns. Every time I delivered a lesson, it went smoothly because they listened attentively. They were quite competitive but in a positive manner. I also taught in different rooms since the cooperating teacher I was under was a science major. So, every day, I had to rotate and teach science to them. Through this, I was able to meet other pupils and adjust to their level of thinking. I also taught English, MAPEH, and ESP..

Week 3 & 4

Through this, I was able to meet other pupils and adjust to their level of thinking. I also taught English, MAPEH, and ESP.

Week 4: Returning to my original cooperating teacher, I was given the opportunity to teach all subjects throughout the day. It includes LRCP, ESP, English, Filipino, MTB-MLE, Mathematics, Araling Panlipunan and MAPEH. It was enjoyable because I had full authority over managing the class, with the cooperating teacher observing on the side. Through this experience, I learned more about my pupils' capabilities and how to adjust accordingly. I made sure to cater to all their needs and different intelligences, avoiding focusing on one aspect. It became apparent to me how taxing being an educator can be, but also how rewarding it is when you see your pupils learning and growing because of your efforts.
